The visualization shows a country's life expectancy on the y axis and annual per capita health expenditure (in inflation-adjusted dollars) on the x axis, over the period 1970-2013. The trajectory of the US is highlighted in red.
The US consistently lags behind...
The US stands out as an outlier: it spends far more on health than any other country, yet the life expectancy of Americans is actually shorter than in other countries that spend far less. Since the mid-1980s, the US has followed a relatively flat trajectory.
The data is highly reflective of health care system quality...
The countries with the highest life expectancies are also the ones with the top-ranked health care systems. Examples include Japan, Switzerland, and Luxembourg.
